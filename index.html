Abstract

Recent advances in diffusion models have enabled post-treatment video generation (PVG), offering richer and more intuitive previews of orthodontic outcomes compared to static visualization. However, paired pre-treatment and post-treatment data are extremely difficult to collect, and existing methods rely on 3D-to-2D contour projections for surrogate supervision. This setup introduces a pronounced domain gap between the 3D-derived contours used for training and the 2D segmentations used during inference, leading to degraded generalization. Moreover, speech-driven mouth motion exhibits rapid, highly non-rigid deformation, making temporal alignment challenging and often resulting in flickering or geometric drift. To address these challenges, we propose the Temporally Consistent Post-orthodontic Video Generation (TCPVG) framework—a diffusion-based approach that synthesizes temporally stable and photorealistic post-treatment videos from speech inputs. TCPVG incorporates a Dual-Encoder Frequency Fusion Network that adaptively bridges the contour-style domain gap via multi-scale frequency fusion, and a Flow-guided Temporal Attention mechanism that leverages dense optical flow and step-wise adaptive fusion to enhance motion coherence under fast articulations. Extensive experiments demonstrate that TCPVG achieves superior perceptual fidelity and temporal consistency over state-of-the-art baselines, establishing a new benchmark for realistic, speech-driven orthodontic video generation.
