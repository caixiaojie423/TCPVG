TCPVG: Temporally Consistent Post-orthodontic Video Generation via Frequency and Flow Fusion

WenjieCai, Yan Tian,
📄 Paper 💻 Code 🎥 Demo

Abstract

Recent advances in diffusion models have made post-orthodontic video generation (PVG) feasible, offering richer previews of treatment outcomes than static images. However, PVG still faces two key challenges: a domain gap between the 3D tooth-model contours used during training and the 2D segmentations available at inference, and rapid, highly non-rigid speech-driven oral motions that degrade temporal consistency. To address these issues, We propose Temporally Consistent Post-orthodontic Video Generation (TCPVG), a diffusion-based framework that, given a talking video, synthesizes a temporally coherent post-orthodontic result. TCPVG incorporates a Dual-Encoder Frequency-Fusion network that performs adaptive frequency-domain fusion to mitigate the 3D-to-2D domain gap, and introduces a flow-guided temporal-attention module that leverages dense optical flow with step-adaptive fusion to enhance stability under fast oral movements. Extensive experiments demonstrate that TCPVG surpasses strong baselines in both perceptual fidelity and temporal coherence.
